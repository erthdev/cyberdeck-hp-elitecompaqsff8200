# Log 04 (May 9, 2026)

This log is the one where the cyberdeck stops being a plan that keeps getting revised and starts being a plan that can actually be executed.

The original cyberdeck concept was built around the wrong hardware from the start. An HP Compaq 8200 motherboard is 27 by 21 centimeters. It needs a proprietary power chain with custom adapter harnesses just to boot. Its CPU has a 65 to 95 watt TDP. None of that belongs in a compact portable enclosure. I kept trying to engineer around those constraints instead of asking whether the hardware was right for the job at all. It was not hahaha.

The reset happened in two parts.

The first part was the hardware decision. A mini PC board running an Intel Celeron 2950M is everything the HP 8200 was not for this use case. Low power draw. Compact form factor. Simple power input from a standard DC brick. No proprietary connectors. No adapter harnesses. No custom wiring just to get a POST screen. It is not fast by any modern standard but that was never the point. The point was always to build something portable and functional that proves the concept with your own hands.

The second part was the enclosure decision. A cleaned-up butane stove case. It sounds strange until you think about what a cyberdeck actually is, its a functional computer built into an unexpected shell. The butane case is compact, already has a rigid structure, has a defined internal cavity to work with, and is something nobody else is using as a PC enclosure. That is exactly the cyberdeck spirit. It is not a project box from an electronics store. It is a repurposed object that becomes something it was never designed to be.

These two decisions together, the right board and the right enclosure, are what the first three logs were building toward without knowing it. The project is smaller now in physical size but it is more honest about what it is trying to do.

The HP 8200 is not gone. It became Bakal-Gubat, which is an open-air wood PC build with dark hardwood shelves and threaded rod columns. That project has its own repository and its own identity now. Same origin, completely different destination.

This repository from this log forward is the cyberdeck only. The butane case. The Celeron board. The proof of concept.

## What happened in this log

* Confirmed the Intel Celeron 2950M mini PC as the correct board for this project
* Selected a repurposed butane stove case as the cyberdeck enclosure
* Separated the HP 8200 into its own project (Bakal-Gubat)
* Redefined the scope and goal of this repository going forward
* Updated the README to reflect the reset direction

## What I need to verify before sourcing the board
* SO-DIMM RAM voltage - must be DDR3L at 1.35V, not standard DDR3 at 1.5V
* Storage format on the mini PC board - 2.5in SATA or M.2 slot
* Exact internal dimensions of the butane stove case - length, width, depth of usable cavity
* Mini PC board dimensions - to confirm fit inside the case
* Power adapter output voltage and connector type for the mini PC board
